Ad Size: Native Marquee Card - Best practices

Imagery Best Practices

Dos

  • Do use photography with vibrant colors and good contrast.
  • Give travelers a sense of what it’s like to be “there”, experiencing the destination/activity/landmark.
  • People may be present, but they must be a part of the landscape and not the focus.
  • Do use images that are high resolution and free of pixelation and blurriness.
  • Do use images that leverage natural light whenever appropriate.

Don’ts

  • Images with people, animals and food as the primary subject.
  • Images with over-saturation, washed-out, heavy shadows, aggressive filters (dark, motion, blur) or any Instagram-style filter treatments.
  • Images that include text, are low resolution, blurry or pixelated.
  • Fish-eye or distorted image lenses or treatments, including lens flare.
  • Solid colors, texture backgrounds, illustrations, graphics or brand-icon centric images.
 

Content Best Practices

Dos

  • Highlight key traveler influencers and delighters.
  • Speaks to the traveler as if they are a real person, not a target market or audience “Your kids will love” not “Families will love”.
  • Informative and conveys unique facets of information about things to do, see, and experience in the destination.
  • Refers to the traveler in second person.
  • Uses gender-neutral language.
  • Speaks to the user’s identity as a participant in the interest.
  • Ad copy should connect with marquee hero image.
  • Call to Action should be clear and connect with headline and imagery.
  • Use sentence case; No all caps or title case.
  • Periods are ok at the end of the sentence.
  • Do include *Terms apply on banners when there is a savings offer (Save up to xx%). For offers such as “free first checked bag” or something similar, if it truly is offered on all purchases, and not on specific flights (or rooms), then it is not necessary. It needs to fit within the max character count set for marquee offer text.

Don’ts

  • Don't use superscript.
  • Don't use exclamation points.
  • Don't use exclamation points [!], or Ampersand [&] should be spelled out as 'and', or quotation marks [" "] in the text.
 

Important:

Please make sure that the language used on your ads matches the point of sale selected (ex: French should be used if you select Expedia France for your campaign).
