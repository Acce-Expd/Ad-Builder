Imagery Best Practices

  • Maintain a consistent customer journey by ensuring the Tile's image and content relate to the content on the landing page.
  • Images with people are not allowed.
  • Images with close up of buildings with no clear view of destination are not allowed.
 

Content Best Practices

Dos: Title

  • Do use sentence case.
  • Do feature a destination name, activity or Point of Interest in the title (e.g. Oahu Snorkeling, Summer Road Trip, Glacier Bay).
  • Do connect the title to the description and image.
  • Do stay within the required character count of 36 (with spaces).

Don’ts: Title

  • Don’t use brand names or taglines.
  • Don't use exclamation points or '&' (should be spelled out as 'and’).
 

Dos: Description

  • Do use sentence case.
  • Do highlight key traveler influencers and delighters (see Traveler Journey map).
  • Do speaks to the traveler as if she is a real person, not a target market or audience “Your kids will love” not “Families will love”.
  • Do use copy that is informative and conveys unique facets of information about things to do, see, and experience in the destination.
  • Do refers to the traveler in second person.
  • Do use gender-neutral language.
  • Do speak to the user’s identity as a participant in the interest.
  • Do connect the description copy to the Title and Image.
  • Do stay within the required character count of 90 (with spaces).
  • Do include Terms apply on banners when there is a *savings offer (Save up to xx%). For offers such as “free first checked bag” or something similar, if it truly is offered on all purchases, and not on specific flights (or rooms), then it is not necessary.

Don’ts: Description

  • Don’t use destination city and state (i.e: Perth, Western Australia)
 

Important:

Please make sure that the language used on your ads matches the point of sale selected (ex: French should be used if you select Expedia France for your campaign).
